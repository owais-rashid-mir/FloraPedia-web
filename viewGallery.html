Ohia lehua

Ohia lehua
Metrosideros polymorpha (MYRTACEAE)

There may be no more widely recognized native Hawaiian tree than the beloved `Ohi`a Lehua. This beautiful member of the myrtle family earned its species name because it assumes many forms, depending upon where it grows.

Crinum asiaticum var. pedunculatum

Crinum asiaticum var. pedunculatum

Crinum pedunculatum is a large bulbous perennial herb, which may reach 2m high. The leaves are strappy and up to 2 m long by 15 cm wide, with blunt points. The flowers are about 10 cm across and borne in clusters of 10-25.

Kokia

Kokia drynarioides Kokia drynarioides is a tree that grows up to 10 m tall. The leaves can be from 12-25 cm wide and have 7-9 lobes with a cordate (heart shaped) leaf base. Kokia flowers are borne individually in the leaf axils near the end of the branches. They are large with showy, brick-red petals that are 10-15 cm long.
